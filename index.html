Work in Progress

Misallocation in Banking

Solo-authored (draft coming soon)

We investigate how bank market power generates misallocation in the banking sector by distorting the allocation of deposits and loans across banks. We develop a model of heterogeneous banks in which the welfare consequences of these distortions are summarized by a set of sufficient statistics based on the weighted dispersion of marginal surplus. Calibrating the model to U.S. banking data, we estimate that the total surplus loss from banking misallocation was approximately 0.8% of total assets annually in the 1980s, and has fallen sharply in recent years. Leveraging the two-sided nature of banks, we decompose total misallocation into deposit-side, loan-side, and interaction components. Finally, we use the framework to discuss the effects of bank mergers and monetary policy on allocative efficiency in banking.
